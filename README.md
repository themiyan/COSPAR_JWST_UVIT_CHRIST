# README for **COSPAR_JWST_UVIT_CHRIST**

## “A Hands‑on Workshop with JWST & UVIT”

Organised by the Committee on Space Research (COSPAR) in collaboration with Christ (Deemed to be University), Bengaluru
Date: **20–31 October 2025**
Venue: Department of Physics & Electronics, Centre of Excellence in Astronomy & Astrophysics, Christ University, Bengaluru

---

## 1. Overview

This repository contains materials from the COSPAR Capacity Building Workshop titled *“A Hands-on Workshop with JWST & UVIT”*, held at Christ (Deemed to be University), Bengaluru from **20–31 October 2025**.

This two-week workshop provided researchers, faculty members, postdoctoral fellows, PhD scholars, and master's students from Asia and surrounding regions with practical experience in astrophysical research using data from the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) and the Ultra Violet Imaging Telescope (UVIT). The event featured expert lectures, intensive tutorials, interactive projects, and collaborative exercises, empowering participants to effectively utilize public JWST and UVIT datasets for advanced astrophysical research. ([cosparindia2025.christuniversity.in](https://cosparindia2025.christuniversity.in/home))

---

## 2. Workshop Structure

* **Week 1**: Detailed lectures on JWST and UVIT science drivers, instrument characteristics, and observational techniques along with interactive, hands-on sessions on data retrieval, processing, analysis, and modeling with JWST and UVIT datasets, 
* **Week 2**: Participant-driven research projects.

Participants were selected based on proficiency in astronomy and Python programming to ensure meaningful scientific engagement.

## 4. Key Topics and Learning Outcomes

The repository covers workshop content across these primary areas:

* JWST and UVIT instrumentation, observing strategies, and public archives
* Data reduction, analysis pipelines, and statistical methods
* Project-based exploration in star formation, exoplanet studies, galaxy evolution, and transient phenomena
* Techniques for deriving scientific results from publicly available astronomical data

Participants gained skills to:

1. Efficiently navigate JWST and UVIT archives and data pipelines
2. Conduct independent analysis of UV/IR astrophysical datasets
3. Design and pursue research questions using JWST and UVIT data
4. Leverage international collaborations fostered through workshop networks

## 6. Repository Contents

Workshop materials have been structured into the following folders for easy navigation:

* `General_Meterial`: Introductory and reference materials relevant to the entire workshop.
* `Hands_On_Imaging`: Hands-on tutorials and exercises focusing on imaging data from JWST.
* `Hands_On_Coronography`: Practical exercises related to coronographic observations with JWST.
* `Hands_On_MAST`: Tutorials on data retrieval and archival searches using the Mikulski Archive for Space Telescopes (MAST).
* `Hands_On_DJA`: Data analysis and tutorials specifically focused on DAWN JWST Archive.
* `Hands_On_MOS`: Interactive sessions covering Multi-Object Spectroscopy (MOS) techniques and data analysis with JWST.
* `Hands_On_IFU`: Tutorials and workflows for handling Integral Field Unit (IFU) spectroscopy data.
* `Hands_On_UVIT`: Practical tutorials and resources for UVIT data analysis and interpretation.
* `Project_Presentations`: Final participant projects and presentations showcasing outcomes from the workshop.


Materials are provided solely for non-commercial, academic, and research purposes. Please ensure proper citation if used externally.
